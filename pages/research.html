The goal of my research is to better understand how carbon moves throughout space and time by physical, biological, and chemical processes within tidal marsh ecosystems. The methodology of my research involves the development of numerical models that provide mechanistic insight into how these processes affect the spatial and temporal distribution of carbon and how the carbon cycling system functions as a whole within these ecosystems. I parameterize these models with field, lab, and historical environmental and GIS data and validate them with those same types of data and statistical analysis.

The carbon cycle in tidal marsh ecosystems is so complex and variable and can not be perfectly described by models within our current understanding and computational abilities. However, with the development of models that concentrate on unclear processes and idealize and simplify other processes that are better understood a more acute understanding of those unclear processes can develop. What was once veiled in confusion can now be articulated and data can be better explained. This is what I hope to acheive.
